Pricing & Open Source Status

LM Studio is committed to making local AI accessible to everyone.

Completely Free for Personal & Business Use

As of recent updates, LM Studio is **free to use for both personal experimentation and internal business purposes**. There are no subscription fees, usage charges, or hidden costs for its core functionalities. This aligns with its mission to democratize access to powerful AI models.

Understanding its Open Source Nature:

  • The **LM Studio GUI application itself is proprietary**. This means its source code is not openly available for modification or redistribution.
  • However, LM Studio is built upon and **heavily leverages leading open-source libraries** like `llama.cpp` (for running GGUF models) and Apple's `MLX` (for Apple Silicon Macs).
  • Furthermore, key components such as LM Studio's **CLI (`lms`), Core SDKs (Python, TypeScript), and the MLX inferencing engine are indeed open source** (MIT licensed), allowing developers to build upon its foundational elements.
  • This model allows LM Studio to provide a polished, user-friendly experience while contributing to and benefiting from the broader open-source AI ecosystem.

How to Get Started

Getting started with LM Studio is simple and straightforward.

Step 1: Download & Install

Visit the official LM Studio website and download the installer for your operating system (macOS, Windows, or Linux). Follow the on-screen instructions to complete the installation.

Download Here

Step 2: Discover & Download Models

Open LM Studio, navigate to the "Discover" tab. Browse the extensive catalog of LLMs, select your preferred model (e.g., a LLaMA or Mistral variant), and click to download its GGUF weights to your local machine.

Step 3: Load & Chat

Go to the "Chat" tab, select the model you just downloaded from the dropdown menu to load it into memory. Once loaded, you can immediately start conversing with the LLM in the chat interface.
